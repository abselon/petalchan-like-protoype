## Short Summary

Petal Chan is a high-contrast, privacy-led app marketing page for an offline period tracker. Its main design behavior is playful but direct: oversized emotional headline, privacy-first copy, app-store CTAs, repeated proof/review anchors, phone imagery, and a long FAQ-style lower page optimized around search queries.

## Key Findings

- The core positioning is privacy and offline use: “Private by design, offline by default,” with “No internet required” and “No account needed” as immediate trust claims [1].
- The page sells against common period-tracker pain points: cloud syncing, account creation, government/third-party access, subscription fatigue, and data portability [1].
- The “shenanigans” worth borrowing are structural rather than purely decorative: punchy headline copy, app-store badges, a “Built Different” interlude, proof/review links, and a big FAQ stack with blunt user questions [1].
- The product-specific modules to prototype should include cycle phase, offline/private status, upcoming dates, widgets, import/export, BBT charting, cervical fluid/LH logging, biometric blur, and food tips [1].
- SEO copy is visibly part of the page strategy: the lower sections repeat “offline period tracker app,” “premium period tracker app,” “one time purchase,” “GenZ,” “modern UI,” and “no data collection” language [1].
